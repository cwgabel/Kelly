Nourish, Protect, Energize: A Brain-Healthy Diet Summary

Core Principles

  • Nourish: Whole foods, including some animal products, provide all essential nutrients.
  • Protect: Avoid inflammatory foods like refined carbs and vegetable oils.
  • Energize: Maintain stable glucose and insulin for optimal brain energy.

Personalization

  • Metabolic health: Carb tolerance varies; insulin resistance may require a ketogenic approach.
  • Immune/gut health: Environmental toxins can impair food tolerance; trial-and-error may be needed.

Ketogenic Diet & Mental Health

  • Originally used for epilepsy (1921).
  • Reduces inflammation, oxidative stress, and insulin resistance.
  • Helps restore brain chemical balance and improves energy metabolism.

Key Study Findings

  • 31 treatment-resistant mental illness patients tried keto under medical supervision.
  • 43% achieved clinical remission; 64% reduced psychiatric meds.
  • Metabolic health improved in all participants.

Why Keto Works

  • Ketosis stabilizes appetite and hormone levels.
  • Reduces cravings, mental "roller coaster," and food addiction.
  • Insulin is the key hormone—lowering it enables fat burning and ketone production.

Common Misconceptions

  • Keto isn’t a specific food list—it’s about managing insulin.
  • Carnivore diets can provide all essential nutrients; fiber is not required for gut health.

ADHD and Diet

  • Older studies showed 60–80% improvement in children on "few foods" diets.
  • New studies in 2025 (Oxford & Michigan) will explore keto for ADHD in adults.

Case Studies

  • Carl: Lifelong depression and anxiety resolved on a carnivore diet.
  • Patients with bipolar and ADHD saw symptom relief in ketosis.

Final Thoughts

  • Keto can be a transformative tool for mental and metabolic health.
  • Support (medical, nutritional, psychological) enhances success.
  • Curiosity, personalization, and understanding biology are key to healing.
